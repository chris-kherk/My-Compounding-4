Food Allergies

Food allergies can trigger a range of allergic reactions, from mild hives and itching to severe anaphylaxis.

The most common food allergens, often referred to as the “big eight,” are responsible for the majority of food allergies. These eight allergens include:

  • Allergic reactions to cow’s milk protein,
  • Egg allergies,
  • Peanut allergies,
  • Tree nuts (including almonds, cashews, walnuts, etc.),
  • Soy allergies,
  • Fish Allergies,
  • Wheat allergies (which are different from gluten intolerance or celiac disease) and
  • Shellfish allergies, which are divided into two groups: crustaceans (e.g., shrimp, crab, lobster) and mollusks (e.g., clams, mussels, oysters).

Avoiding allergenic foods is crucial, such as but accidents can happen, and that’s where compounded medication by My Compounding Pharmacy come into play.
